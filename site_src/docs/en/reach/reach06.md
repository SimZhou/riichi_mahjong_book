# Riichi Theory (2)

## Cut Inside, Extend Outside

For example, if you have a shape like <img src="../../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34">,

the ordinary play is to cut <img src="../../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34"> and aim for a future ryanmen by drawing <img src="../../hai/sou5.gif" width="24" height="34">.

However, if you are going to declare riichi, the story changes.

## Example 1

<img src="../../hai/man1.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/man2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/man2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/man2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/man3.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou6.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou8.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sha.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sha.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sha.gif" width="24" height="34"> Draw <img src="../../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34"> Dora <img src="../../hai/sou6.gif" width="24" height="34">

This hand will usually go for riichi.

If you are going to riichi, the theory is to cut <img src="../../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34">.

If a genbutsu <img src="../../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34"> appears in the pond,
then <img src="../../hai/sou3.gif" width="24" height="34"> becomes easier for opponents to discard.

That said, the effect is not overwhelming.

If <img src="../../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34"> is clearly more dangerous,
then riichi after cutting <img src="../../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34"> is also completely fine.

In Example 2, you can riichi after cutting either <img src="../../hai/pin2.gif" width="24" height="34"> or <img src="../../hai/pin3.gif" width="24" height="34">.

But in a hand like this, which cannot become pinfu anyway,
you should riichi in a way that keeps the chance to make an ankou.

If you later draw another <img src="../../hai/pin1.gif" width="24" height="34">,
you can declare ankan.

That raises the fu,
and you may even pick up extra dora.

## Keep the Information Minimal

The old idea of camouflage discards,
where you try to set traps in your discard row,
is now viewed very negatively.

The standard view today is the opposite:
the less distinctive your discard row is, the better.

## Example 3

<img src="../../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin6.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin7.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin8.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou3.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou8.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/sou9.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pei.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pei.gif" width="24" height="34"> Draw <img src="../../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34"> Dora <img src="../../hai/sou9.gif" width="24" height="34">

This is a very typical riichi hand.

Here you should move the pair from <img src="../../hai/pei.gif" width="24" height="34"> to <img src="../../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34">,
then cut <img src="../../hai/pei.gif" width="24" height="34"> and riichi.

If you cut <img src="../../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34"> here,
then once you riichi, other players will find it easier to discard
<img src="../../hai/pin1.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin2.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin3.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pin7.gif" width="24" height="34">.

If you cut <img src="../../hai/pei.gif" width="24" height="34"> instead,
opponents can tell almost nothing except that you are not on a tanki wait.

The ideal riichi discard row is one where terminals and honors line up naturally and reveal as little character as possible.

For example:

<img src="../../hai/nan.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/pei.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/tyun.gif" width="24" height="34"><img src="../../hai/yman1.gif" width="32" height="24">

When you face a riichi pond like that,
you often have no choice but to push.

More than clumsy camouflage,
this kind of <span class="b">strong pond</span> is what actually makes ron wins easier.
