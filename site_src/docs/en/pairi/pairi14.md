# Tile Theory at One-Shanten (2)

First, let us look at hands with too many blocks.

If you have fully absorbed everything up to this point, you should be able to derive the correct discard in every case on this page.

**Example 4**  
<img src="../hai/man1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou9.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Tsumo <img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" />

=> Discard <img src="../hai/man1.gif" width="24" height="34" />

Both choices have the same acceptance count, 12 tiles, and both leave a kanchan wait. So the difference is not there.

Then what about the number of improvements into a better shape?

The manzu side only has one good-shape improvement: drawing <img src="../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34" /> to make a ryan-kan shape.

The pinzu side, by contrast, can improve with <img src="../hai/pin3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin9.gif" width="24" height="34" />, which is much richer.  
(Shanpon improvements are equivalent here, so they are excluded.)

There is a slight difference in how easy it is to win on kan <img src="../hai/man2.gif" width="24" height="34" /> versus kan <img src="../hai/pin6.gif" width="24" height="34" />, but the orthodox choice is still to value the number of improvements and drop the manzu side.

**Example 5**  
<img src="../hai/pin2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou6.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou8.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Tsumo <img src="../hai/pin5.gif" width="24" height="34" />

=> Discard <img src="../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" />

This is a choice between two ryanmen taatsu.

It is a shape-reading mistake to think the <img src="../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou8.gif" width="24" height="34" /> side is worse just because you already use one <img src="../hai/sou6.gif" width="24" height="34" /> yourself.

By discarding <img src="../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" />, the souzu side can become a **ryanmen plus kanchan** composite shape.

**Example 6**  
<img src="../hai/man4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou5.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Tsumo <img src="../hai/man9.gif" width="24" height="34" />

=> Discard <img src="../hai/man4.gif" width="24" height="34" /> (or <img src="../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34" />)

At first glance, it may look as though you could discard manzu, pinzu, or souzu.

However, if you drop the manzu side, then the reverse draw <img src="../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34" /> still remains an effective tile.

☆ <img src="../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/pin5.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/sou5.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Tsumo <img src="../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34" />

If the table situation does not create a special difference, the correct move is to drop <img src="../hai/man4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../hai/man5.gif" width="24" height="34" /> first.

This is a fairly fine point, so missing it is not a major disaster. Even so, keep the idea of <span class="red"><strong>covering a reverse draw</strong></span> somewhere in the back of your mind.
