# How to Handle Dora (1)

Dora are valuable because simply holding them raises the value of your hand.  
That is exactly why it is important to learn how to play without wasting them.

**Example 1**

In a hand like this, if you casually cut <img src="../../hai/pin2m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" /> just because it looks like an Iipeikou shape,

then you can no longer use the dora.

If you cut <img src="../../hai/pin5m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" /> instead,  
you can still swap <img src="../../hai/pin4m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" /> out for the dora later.  
To avoid this kind of basic mistake, always keep in mind what you will do if you draw the dora.

Now one of your pairs has become unnecessary,  
so you can break either one.

But if you think about dora acceptance,  
you should cut <img src="../../hai/pin4m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />.

<img src="../../hai/man1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/hatu.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/hatu.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/hatu.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/nan.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Tsumo <img src="../../hai/man1.gif" width="24" height="34" />

Then if you draw the dora,  
you can switch the head by cutting <img src="../../hai/man4m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />.

Some people might think:

"Even if I cut <img src="../../hai/man4m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />, couldn't I just leave <img src="../../hai/man1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man3.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man4.gif" width="24" height="34" /> floating as it is?"

But leaving tiles around the dora suji floating is dangerous,  
and if you later draw <img src="../../hai/man7m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />, <img src="../../hai/man8m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />, <img src="../../hai/pin7m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />, or <img src="../../hai/pin8m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />,  
you will once again be forced to choose between keeping or throwing away the dora acceptance.  
So that line is incorrect.

**Example 4**

<img src="../../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/man6.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin1.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/pin9.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/sou2.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/sou4.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/sou7.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/sou8.gif" width="24" height="34" /><img src="../../hai/nan.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Tsumo <img src="../../hai/pin2.gif" width="24" height="34" /> Dora <img src="../../hai/pin3.gif" width="24" height="34" />

This is a one-shanten hand that is holding a safety tile.

I think the correct play is to keep <img src="../../hai/pin2m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />  
and discard the safety tile instead.  
Locking the shape in too early with a hand like this is not good play.

If you draw the dora <img src="../../hai/pin3m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" />,  
not only does the hand become more valuable,  
your acceptance also doubles from 12 tiles to 24 tiles.

Of course, I understand the concern that later you may no longer be able to discard <img src="../../hai/pin2m.gif" width="19" height="26" class="hai" /> safely.  
But this hand is not high-value to begin with, and once you fall behind it is hard to fight back anyway.  
If someone else declares riichi first, you can just fold honestly.

### Theory Summary

<span class="green">
When you discard, count your dora acceptance as well.<br />
Especially in hands that lack both value and acceptance, you should not throw dora acceptance away.
</span>
