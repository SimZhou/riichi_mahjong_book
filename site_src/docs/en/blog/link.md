# Mahjong Link Directory

This page organizes mahjong-related sites listed in the original site's `link/` section.

## Mahjong Strategy and Rules Sites

1. [Mahjong Rules and Yaku Explained: A Beginner-Friendly Mahjong Course](http://www2.odn.ne.jp/~cbm15900/)
   A site that explains mahjong rules and play through a conversational format. It has a very distinctive style and is built with great care. If this site feels a little difficult, this is a good place to start.

2. [Monthly Online Mahjong](http://magazine.mahjong-rule.com/)
   A virtual online mahjong magazine collecting blog updates and guest columns from well-known players.

3. [Henachoko Mahjong Club](http://www.geocities.co.jp/HeartLand-Himawari/2779/)
   Covers mahjong rules, score calculation, and online mahjong play. A carefully made site that is easy to recommend.

4. [Mahjong Rules / Free Mahjong](http://www.mahjong-rule.jp/)
   A very detailed site explaining mahjong rules and actual gameplay.

5. [Mahjong Paradise Palace](http://www1.clovernet.ne.jp/silver_x/index.htm)
   Includes strategy lectures, plus recommendations for mahjong manga and DVDs.

6. [Mahjong Empire](../kihon/kihon06.md)
   A general information site where you can download mahjong materials. This rebuilt site also borrowed tile image assets from it.

7. [Competitive Mahjong Circle Iishanten](http://www.mahjong.to/)
   The homepage of a famous competitive mahjong circle. This site also provided source materials for the rebuild.

8. [Collected Real Mahjong Essays](http://www.geocities.jp/pyutaro1973/top.htm)
   The classic piece *Gate of Mahjong* in particular is well worth reading. It may change the way you think about mahjong.

9. [Mahjong Percent](http://mjclv.com/)
   Offers strength tests and explanations of winning methods. The site design is clean and pleasant to read.

10. [Mahjong no Janryu.com](http://www.mj-dragon.com/)
    Covers practical rules, yaku lists, score calculation, and free mahjong game information, while the blog section also posts news.

11. [Hiii's Mahjong Research](http://www.ix3.jp/hiii/)
    A site that studies mahjong through mathematical methods. It also serialized articles in *Kindai Mahjong*.

12. [Ara's Mahjong Laboratory](http://mahjong.ara3.net/)
    A logic-oriented mahjong research site where you can also download a solo mahjong practice tool.

13. [Ruu's Mahjong Strategy Notes](http://www.faruzeru.com/)
    Tests strategy using game records and statistics, while also providing rich rules content.

14. [Mahjong Kingdom](http://www.mj-king.net/)
    A rational strategy site that also provides tools such as a solo mahjong practice machine.

15. [Shingo's Column](http://www.ne.jp/asahi/shingo/teilwall/)
    A mahjong column written by a top Tenhou player. The theory is detailed and worth reading carefully.

16. [Modern Mahjong Theory](http://yabejp.web.fc2.com/mahjong/index.html)
    Outstanding both in quality and scope, and one of the strongest strategy sites around. It gives a systematic introduction to newer mahjong theory.

17. [MahjongWalker](http://www.mahjong.or.jp/)
    A quasi-official site gathering a large amount of mahjong information and recognized by professional organizations.

18. [Joo](http://www.mahjong-joo.com/)
    A support site for women professional mahjong players.

## Mahjong Parlors

1. [Mahjong ZOO](http://www.mj-zoo.jp/index.htm)
   The official site of the Mahjong ZOO parlor chain.

2. [Mahjong Club Jungle](http://m-jungle.com/)
   The official site of Mahjong Club Jungle.

3. [Janso Search: Nationwide Mahjong Parlor Search](http://jan39.com/)
   Lets you quickly search parlors across Japan, and also includes discard problems and guest columns.

## Mahjong Blogs

1. [Mahjong Research Life Is Going Well](http://majanjan.com/research-life/)
   The blog of Ideal Janshi. It teaches both basic play and includes reviews of mahjong books and manga.

2. [Pon-Chii Recommendations](http://ameblo.jp/mjtextnaki/)
   Fuyuhiko Katsurada's blog, where you can read very high-level calling strategy.

3. [Cut Mahjong Books! Cut Mahjong Games!!](http://ameblo.jp/mj-book/)
   G. Uzak's blog, covering mahjong books, game reviews, and detailed discard analysis.

4. [DeLonge Wants to Master Mahjong Completely](http://delmahjong.blog93.fc2.com/)
   A blog centered on Tenhou games and practical game records.

5. [Discard Problems for Intermediate Players](http://rokubunngi.blog7.fc2.com/)
   The blog of the young online expert Rokubungi, known for high-quality discard problems.

6. [Makoto Fukuchi Blog](http://fukuchi.cocolog-nifty.com/blog/)
   The blog of Makoto Fukuchi, a columnist for *Kindai Mahjong*.

7. [Tonpu Battle Member Log](http://ameblo.jp/mj-member/)
   A blog by Yasunori Suda of the Japan Professional Mahjong Association.

8. [Professional Janshi Kota Yoshida's Sideways Battle Diary](http://blog.livedoor.jp/golden_arm/)
   Rich in tile illustrations and enjoyable both as reading material and as strategy content.

9. [Mahjong Parlor Clerk Quotes version 2.0](http://blog.livedoor.jp/aladdinchance1000/)
   A blog by professional player Koichi Kihara, discussing the essence of mahjong through "choice" and "selection."

10. [Northern Ibaraki HAZ's Mahjong Diary](http://doraaka.exblog.jp/)
    Records long free-play sessions, Tenhou results, and related mahjong notes.

11. [The Strong Players of the Mahjong Board](http://blog.livedoor.jp/eightcut/)
    A roundup of strategy-related comments from the 2ch mahjong board.

12. [Polyhedron: OmoUra Diary Returns](http://polyhedron2.blog65.fc2.com/)
    A blog by the strong Tenhou player Sakurako. Even the non-mahjong entries are enjoyable.

13. [Make Money With Mahjong: How to Win at Free Tables](http://mahjyong.blog64.fc2.com/)
    A strategy blog about winning at free-play tables and online mahjong.

## Professional Mahjong Organizations

1. [Japan Professional Mahjong League](http://www.ma-jan.or.jp/)
   Home to many strong professional players. It also offers columns and discard problems, so it is worth reading even if you are not especially interested in pro mahjong.

2. [Japan Professional Mahjong Association](http://npm2001.com/)
   A younger-skewing professional group with a strong presence in games like MJ4.

3. [Saikouisen Japan Professional Mahjong Association](http://saikouisen.com/)
   One of the oldest professional organizations, known for players such as Iida and Kaneko.

## Free Mahjong Games

1. [Yuyu Space](http://yuyukukan.com/)
   A mahjong game introduction site covering 3D mahjong, online mahjong, and Flash mahjong.

2. [Flash Mahjong Tensei](http://www.web-jong.com/)
   A site where you can directly play Flash mahjong games.

3. [Jan Game](http://www.jangame.jp/jan/)
   A multiplayer Flash mahjong game that does not require downloading or installation.
